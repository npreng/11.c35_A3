Phase One: Sketching Visualizations

My question for visualization was "How does the difference in gender populations vary based on birth year?"

Design Rationale for Sketch 1:

  • This preliminary graph looks like a very nice sine curve so far!
  • At this point, I think it's a good idea to limit my scope into one year of data (2000), because having data from both 2000 and 1900 may be a bit overwhelming / may clutter the visualization
  • I think the numerical difference between gender populations may be more impactful than percent difference or proportion, as it preserves the values in the raw data more exactly and shows human scale
  • I might want to explore what visualizing proportion of each gender might look like
  • I could choose between displaying birth year (more of a timeline) over age, to give a timeline feel for the graphic. This graphic currently has age because including birth year may make the data mislead the reader into thinking the data was collected in that year
  • I definitely want to choose a non blue and pink color scheme to defy gender roles when I'm doing part 2 of the assignment

Design Rationale for Sketch 2:

  • I think the stacked bar chart lets the viewer compare between years a little more accurately than the numerical difference chart
  • Though there is some information loss in terms of the difference counts, the data normalization is really helpful to see trends in proportion that are not affected by population
  • After making this chart, I realized that the percentage of women increases dramatically in the last few age spans, which is a new and exciting finding to highlight!
  • I would want to see if I can incorporate data from the 1900s with this chart, but still don't know how to do this in a way that's not too cluttered
  • I'm not sure how easy it is to see the difference in scale between the 49% women to 75% women in the chart, so I might want to experiment with a visualization that lets you compare proportions a bit easier (Note: I end up doing this in my next sketch)

Design Rationale for Sketch 3:

  • Though I'm not the happiest this visualization, I wanted to make something that allows you to see changes in proportion easily. I chose to use pie charts because I think it's easier for a person to recognize 1/2 and 3/4 of a pie than 1/2 and 3/4 of a bar
  • Since I didn't want 20 different pie charts to compare with, I also thought it would be a good idea to aggregate the data over 10-year age ranges. Aggregating did help make the sketch easier to look at, but there is unfortunately some loss in granularity.
  • I still wanted to easily communicate that the proportion of women drastically increases at the last few age ranges, which I found in the previous sketch, and think that the pie charts are able to provide this information
